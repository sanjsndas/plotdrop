Privacy Policy

Effective: June 27, 2026 · Last updated: June 2026

1. Introduction

Your trust matters Plot Drop Lawn Edging (“we,” “us,” or “our”) respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, interact with our services, or communicate with us.

We adhere to strict data protection principles and comply with applicable privacy laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). This policy applies to all users of our website, clients, and individuals who engage with Plot Drop in any capacity.

By using our website or services, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please refrain from using our site or providing us with your personal data.

Our commitment: We collect only the data we need, process it transparently, and keep it secure. We never sell your personal information.

2. Information We Collect

We collect various types of information to provide and improve our services. This includes:

  • Personal identification data: Name, email address, phone number, postal address, and other contact details you provide when you fill out forms, request a quote, or communicate with us.
  • Project-related data: Property address, measurements, photos, design preferences, and any other details you share about your lawn edging needs.
  • Usage data: IP address, browser type, operating system, pages visited, time spent on pages, and referring URLs. This is collected via cookies and similar technologies.
  • Payment and transaction data: If you make a payment, we may collect billing information (processed securely by our third-party payment processors). We do not store full credit card numbers on our servers.
  • Communication data: Records of your correspondence with us, including emails, phone calls, and chat messages.

We also collect aggregated, anonymized data for analytics and research purposes. This data does not identify any individual.

3. How We Use Your Information

Plot Drop uses your personal information for the following purposes:

  1. Service delivery: To provide lawn edging consultations, estimates, installations, and maintenance services. We use your project details to tailor our work to your property.
  2. Communication: To respond to your inquiries, send appointment reminders, follow up on projects, and share important updates about our services.
  3. Marketing and promotions: With your consent, we may send you newsletters, special offers, and educational content about lawn care and edging. You can opt out at any time.
  4. Improvement and analytics: To analyze website usage, user preferences, and service performance. This helps us enhance our website, user experience, and service offerings.
  5. Legal compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes. We may also use your data to protect our rights, property, or safety.

We process your data based on one or more of the following legal grounds: your consent, the performance of a contract, our legitimate business interests, or compliance with legal obligations.

4. Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. However, we may share your data with:

  • Service providers: Third-party vendors who assist us with payment processing, email delivery, hosting, analytics, and customer support. These providers are contractually obligated to protect your data and use it only for the services they perform for us.
  • Professional advisors: Lawyers, accountants, and consultants who help us with legal, financial, or business matters.
  • Law enforcement and regulators: When required by law, court order, or government regulation, or when we believe disclosure is necessary to prevent harm or fraud.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred to the new entity. We will notify you of any such change.

We require all third parties to respect your privacy and comply with data protection laws. We take reasonable steps to ensure they provide adequate safeguards.

5. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience, analyze site traffic, and remember your preferences. Cookies are small text files stored on your device. We use the following types:

  • Essential cookies: Necessary for the basic functionality of the site (e.g., session management, security).
  • Performance cookies: Collect anonymous data about how you interact with our site (e.g., pages visited, time spent). We use this to improve our website.
  • Functional cookies: Remember your preferences (e.g., language, region) to provide a personalized experience.
  • Marketing cookies: Used with your consent to deliver relevant advertisements and track the effectiveness of our campaigns.

You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings. However, disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. For more details, please see our Cookies Policy.

We use Google Analytics to understand user behavior. Google’s privacy policy applies to their tracking.

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These include:

  • Encryption: Data transmitted between your browser and our website is encrypted using SSL/TLS protocols.
  • Access controls: Only authorized personnel have access to your data, and they are bound by confidentiality agreements.
  • Secure storage: We store data on secure servers with firewalls and intrusion detection systems.
  • Regular audits: We review our security practices periodically and update them as needed.

While we strive to protect your data, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, but we are committed to safeguarding your information to the best of our ability.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, or as required by law. The retention period depends on the type of data and the purpose for which it was collected:

  • Client data: We keep project-related information for up to 7 years after the last service, to handle warranties, follow-ups, and historical records.
  • Marketing data: If you opt in to marketing communications, we retain your contact details until you unsubscribe or request deletion.
  • Usage data: Analytics data is retained for a maximum of 26 months, after which it is aggregated or anonymized.
  • Legal obligations: We may retain data longer to comply with tax, accounting, or regulatory requirements.

When data is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymize it.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: You can correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure (“right to be forgotten”): You can request deletion of your data, subject to legal exceptions.
  • Restriction: You can ask us to limit processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Data portability: You can request a machine-readable copy of your data to transfer to another provider.
  • Objection: You can object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw it at any time.

To exercise your rights, please contact us using the details in the “Contact Us” section below. We will respond to your request within 30 days, as required by law.

We do not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights.

9. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to external sites, such as payment processors, social media platforms, or resource pages. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these third-party sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before providing any personal information.

We may also integrate with third-party services (e.g., Google Maps, YouTube) that have their own data collection practices. These integrations are governed by the privacy policies of the respective providers.

10. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to individuals under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you are a parent or guardian and believe we have inadvertently collected data from a child, please contact us, and we will take steps to delete it.

11. International Transfers

If you are located outside the U.S., your data may be transferred to and processed in the U.S., where data protection laws may differ from those in your jurisdiction. We take appropriate measures to ensure that your data receives an adequate level of protection, including standard contractual clauses where required.

14. Additional Privacy Details

14.1. Automated Decision-Making

We do not use automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you. Any decisions regarding your project are made by our human team members.

14.2. Do-Not-Track Signals

Our website does not respond to Do-Not-Track (DNT) signals from browsers, as there is no consistent industry standard for DNT. However, we respect your cookie preferences through our consent manager.

14.3. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have the right to request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we collect, the purposes of processing, and the third parties with whom we share data. They also have the right to opt out of the sale of their data (we do not sell data) and to request deletion. To exercise these rights, contact us via the details above.

14.4. GDPR Rights (EEA & UK)

For users in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we process data in compliance with the GDPR. You have the right to data portability, to object to processing, and to lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority. Our lead data protection contact is our Privacy Officer, reachable at privacy@plotdrop.com.

14.5. Nevada Privacy Rights

Nevada residents may submit a request to opt out of the sale of their data. As we do not sell data, this request is automatically honored.

14.6. Data Protection Officer

We have appointed a Data Protection Officer (DPO) to oversee our privacy practices. You can contact our DPO at dpo@plotdrop.com.

14.7. Security Incident Response

In the event of a data breach, we have procedures in place to contain, investigate, and notify affected individuals and regulators as required by law.

14.8. Consent to International Processing

By using our services, you consent to the transfer of your data to the United States, where our servers are located. We ensure that adequate safeguards are in place for such transfers.

14.9. Data Minimization

We adhere to the principle of data minimization: we collect only the data that is directly relevant and necessary for the specified purposes.

14.10. Accountability

We are accountable for our data processing activities. We maintain records of processing, conduct privacy impact assessments, and train our staff on privacy best practices.

This comprehensive Privacy Policy reflects our unwavering commitment to protecting your privacy. We believe that transparency and trust are the foundations of a lasting relationship with our clients.
