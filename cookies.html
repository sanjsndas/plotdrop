Effective: June 27, 2026 · Last updated: June 2026

1. Introduction

Your privacy matters At Plot Drop Lawn Edging ("we," "us," "our"), we use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience on our website, analyze user behavior, and deliver personalized content. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and your choices regarding their use.

This policy is part of our broader commitment to transparency and privacy, as outlined in our Privacy Policy. By using our website, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy.

What are cookies? Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, or phone) when you visit a website. They help websites remember your actions and preferences over time.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We use the following categories of cookies on our website:

2.1. Essential (Strictly Necessary) Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the basic functionality of our website. They enable core features such as:

  • Secure login and session management
  • Remembering your cookie consent preferences
  • Maintaining server load balancing and security
  • Enabling basic navigation and page functionality

Without these cookies, our website would not function properly. They do not collect any personal data and are exempt from consent requirements.

2.2. Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how users interact with our website. We use them to:

  • Track page visits, time spent on pages, and user journeys
  • Identify which pages are most popular and which need improvement
  • Monitor website performance and resolve technical issues
  • Aggregate anonymous data for statistical analysis

We primarily use Google Analytics for this purpose. The data collected is anonymous and cannot identify you personally.

2.3. Functional Cookies

Functional cookies allow our website to remember your choices and provide enhanced, personalized features. These include:

  • Remembering your language and region preferences
  • Storing your form data (e.g., consultation request details) for convenience
  • Maintaining your preferences for displaying content (e.g., dark mode)
  • Remembering items in your quote or project basket

These cookies improve your user experience by making the site feel more intuitive and responsive.

2.4. Targeting and Advertising Cookies

With your consent, we use these cookies to deliver relevant advertisements and promotional content. They may also be used by third-party advertisers to:

  • Show you personalized ads based on your browsing history
  • Limit the number of times you see an ad
  • Measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns
  • Retarget you with ads on other platforms (e.g., social media)

We only use these cookies with your explicit consent, and you can withdraw your consent at any time.

3. Third-Party Cookies

Our website may embed content or use services from third-party providers, which may set their own cookies. These include:

  • Google Analytics: Used for web analytics and performance tracking. Google's privacy policy applies to their cookies.
  • YouTube: If we embed videos from YouTube, they may set cookies to remember your preferences and track video views.
  • Google Maps: When displaying location information or interactive maps, Google may set cookies for functionality and analytics.
  • Social media platforms: If you use social sharing buttons (e.g., Facebook, Twitter, Instagram), these platforms may set cookies to enable sharing and track engagement.
  • Payment processors: If you make a payment through our site, our payment partners (e.g., Stripe, PayPal) may set cookies for security and transaction management.

We do not control third-party cookies and are not responsible for their data practices. We recommend reviewing the privacy and cookie policies of these third-party providers.

4. How Long Do Cookies Last?

Cookies can be either:

  • Session cookies: These are temporary and expire when you close your browser. They are used to maintain your session during a single visit.
  • Persistent cookies: These remain on your device for a set period (days, months, or years) or until you manually delete them. They are used to remember your preferences across visits.

The lifespan of our cookies varies depending on their purpose:

  • Essential cookies: session-based (expire when you close your browser)
  • Analytics cookies: up to 26 months (Google Analytics) or as configured
  • Functional cookies: up to 12 months
  • Targeting cookies: up to 30 days to 2 years, depending on the provider

You can view and manage cookie lifespans through your browser settings.

5. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • To provide core functionality: Enable secure login, maintain session state, and ensure website stability.
  • To improve user experience: Remember your preferences, save form data, and provide personalized content.
  • To analyze and optimize: Understand user behavior, identify trends, and make data-driven improvements to our website and services.
  • To market effectively: Deliver relevant advertisements, track campaign performance, and retarget interested users.
  • To support security: Detect fraudulent activity, prevent malicious attacks, and protect user data.

Our use of cookies is balanced with your privacy rights. We only set non-essential cookies with your explicit consent, and you have full control over your cookie preferences.

6.2. Browser Settings

10. Additional Cookie Information

10.1. Cookie Security

We prioritize the security of your data. All cookies we set are transmitted over secure HTTPS connections to prevent interception. We do not store sensitive personal information in cookies. Any personal data collected through cookies is handled in accordance with our Privacy Policy.

10.2. First-Party vs. Third-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are set directly by Plot Drop and are used for site functionality, analytics, and preferences. Third-party cookies are set by external services we integrate with (e.g., Google, Facebook). We have limited control over third-party cookies, and their use is governed by the respective provider's policies.

10.3. Do Not Track (DNT) Signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" feature that sends a signal to websites indicating that you do not want to be tracked. Our website currently does not respond to DNT signals due to the lack of a consistent industry standard. However, we respect your cookie preferences through our consent dialog.

10.4. Cookie Management for Mobile Devices

Mobile devices also allow you to manage cookies. On iOS, you can go to Settings > Safari > Privacy & Security to manage cookies. On Android, you can manage cookies in your browser's settings menu. For third-party app cookies, you may need to adjust settings within the app itself.

10.5. Cookie Consent Evidence

10.6. Opt-Out Mechanisms
